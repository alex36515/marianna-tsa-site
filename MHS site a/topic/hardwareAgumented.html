Augmented Reality Headsets

Augmented reality devices can range from extremely low-tech to extremely high-tech.

The cheapest device available right now is the Polylens. For $30, it's a DIY solution to augmented reality. It's the Google Cardboard of AR. Sabba Keynejad decided that the prices of other augmented reality devices were too high, so he set out to make a more affordably-priced option of his own. Since it's being made by a one man team, there's not much information available as of yet.

On the opposite end of the spectrum, there's devices such as the Microsoft Hololens. Currently priced at $3000 for the Development Edition and $5000 for the Commercial Suite, one can see why Sabba Kenyejad thought that there should be a cheaper option. However, as was the case with the Polylens, with the Hololens, you get what you pay for. The Hololens is so advanced that NASA's Jet Propulsion Lab is using it to explore the surface of Mars.

The Hololens is currently aimed primarily at developers and large corporations who can afford the hefty price tag. That's not to say that there aren't expensive augmented reality devices aimed at consumers. A prime example is Google Glass. They've released very few units because they're still working out what they want the final product to be. Their original release included 8,000 units. All purchasers had to pay $1,500 and fly to a Google office to get the glasses fitted to their heads and to be taught how to use them. Google currently has deals with companies such as Augmate, APX Labs, and Advanced Medical Applications.

Aside from the extremes, there are several medium-priced devices available. One seller of such devices is LAFORGE Optical. They sell have augmented reality glasses available for preorder for $590. For the price, the glasses have some pretty impressive specs.
