About

National TSA (Technology Student Association) is an organization built around the concept of furthering middle and high schoolers interests in different aspects of the STEM fields. Once known as the AIASA (American Industrial Arts Student Association), it is the oldest program focused primarily on students who are enrolled in technologically advanced courses. TSA has been offering children and teens the opportunity to delve deeper into the technical field for nearly four decades. Currently, on the national level, over a quarter of a million students middle school to high school tier students are members of TSA. With about 2000 schools and 2500 teachers spreading the mission of the Technology Student Association nationwide, students who enroll and participate in the program are projected to have a 100% likelihood of graduating from high school. How do true TSA members participate in the expansive association? By attending the annual National TSA conference and taking part in some of the 60 competitive events held there for middle and high school students. These competitions challenge youth's abilities to work by themselves or as a team in areas such as communication, design and engineering, environmental systems, transportation, and manufacturing/construction, to name a few. TSA has truly made a difference in innumerable children's lives since its creation nearly forty years ago. Since then, many states have made their own divisions, such as our very own Florida TSA division. Kids from huge cities to small rural towns have all been given the same opportunity: to learn to lead in a more technical world.
